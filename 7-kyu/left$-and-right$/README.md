<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/53f211b159c3fcec3d000efa/train/javascript target="_blank">Left$   and   Right$</a></h2><h3>7 kyu</h3><p>Once upon a time, more precisely upon BASIC time, there were 2 functions named <code>LEFT$</code> and <code>RIGHT$</code> (I wrote them uppercase because it was the custom in those early years).</p><p>These functions let you read left(/right)-most characters of a string.</p><ul><li>use:  <code>LEFT$ (str, i)</code>  -&gt; returns <code>i</code> left-most characters of <code>str</code>.</li></ul><pre><code>- eg:A$="ABCDEFG":PRINT LEFT$(A$,3) - prints ABC</code></pre><p>and <code>RIGHT$ (str, i )</code>,  of course, returns <code>i</code> right-most characters of <code>str</code>.</p><h3 id="so-your-mission-is">So, your mission is...</h3><p>...to write 2 functions that will work as the BASIC ones did... except :</p><ul><li>if <code>i</code> is an integer:<ul><li><code>i</code> may be a negative integer.
